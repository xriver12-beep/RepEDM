Newsletter Management System (NMS)A comprehensive email marketing platform supporting professional newsletter design, delivery, subscriber management, and statistical analysis.Core FeaturesNewsletter Design: Drag-and-drop editor supporting block assembly and raw HTML source editing.Subscriber Management: Full member database, segmentation, bulk data correction, and automated unsubscription management (Soft Delete).Precise Audience Calculation: Automatically excludes invalid, unsubscribed, or deleted records before sending; provides detailed audience composition analysis (Total vs. Active).Automated Delivery: Scheduled sending and trigger-based emails.Analytics & Reporting: Analysis of Open Rates, Click-Through Rates (CTR), and Unsubscribe Rates with visualized reports and detailed unsubscription logs.Tracking System: Built-in tracking pixels and link replacement mechanisms to monitor user behavior accurately.Asset Management: Centralized management for images (JPG/PNG/GIF/SVG) and documents (PDF/DOC) with category tagging and usage tracking.Access Control: Multi-level user permission management.Traffic & Rate Control: Built-in IP warming, ISP-specific delivery throttling, and Smart Queueing to ensure high deliverability and avoid spam flagging.Role DescriptionsThe system defines various user roles. The primary differences between a Manager and an Approver are as follows:1. ManagerPositioning: System administrative level, typically a department head or team leader.Permissions:Backend Access: Can access the system backend to view user lists and system-wide statistics.Data Visibility: Can view detailed profiles of subordinates and other users.Approval Duties: Responsible for reviewing applications submitted by direct reports.Target Users: Department heads, Team Leaders.2. ApproverPositioning: Functional role focused on specific business process sign-offs.Permissions:Backend Access: No access to the system backend (permissions equivalent to a standard User).Data Visibility: Can only view data related to themselves or assigned for their review.Approval Duties: Responsible for reviewing assigned stages (e.g., Finance or Legal review); no hierarchical relationship with the submitter is required.Target Users: Finance staff, Legal counsel, General Affairs, or specific process owners.FeatureManagerApproverBackend AccessAuthorizedUnauthorizedApproval LogicDirect Supervisor (Hierarchical)Assigned Stage (Functional)Data PermissionsHigh (Subordinate data visible)Standard (Context-specific only)System Behavior Documentation1. Subscriber DeletionTo maintain data integrity and prevent Foreign Key Constraint errors, the system utilizes a Soft Delete mechanism:Action: "Deleting" a subscriber does not physically remove the record from the database.Status: The subscriber's status field is updated to deleted.Impact: Deleted subscribers are automatically excluded from all future campaigns. Historical logs and statistics are preserved for reporting accuracy.2. Campaign Deletion PermissionsAdmin: Full authority to delete any campaign.Manager: Authorized to delete campaigns within the system.Creator: Regardless of role, the original creator always has the right to delete their own campaigns.Standard User: Cannot delete campaigns created by others.3. Traffic ControlIP Warm-up: For new or dormant IPs, the system limits daily volume, increasing it by a factor of 1.5x daily until full capacity is reached. Overflow is automatically rescheduled.Throttling: Limits sending rates per ISP (e.g., Gmail: 100/min, Yahoo: 50/min) to avoid 421/451 errors and temporary blocks.Smart Queueing: Uses intelligent polling. If a specific domain reaches its rate limit, the system pauses that domain's queue and prioritizes other domains to maximize throughput.4. Unsubscribe MechanismDual-Check: Monitors two dedicated mailboxes: noedm@winton.com.tw and Unsubscribe@winton.com.tw.Comprehensive Scan: Scans Inbox, Junk, Spam, and a custom EDM_unsubscribe folder.Keyword Recognition: Identifies multilingual keywords (e.g., "unsubscribe", "退訂") to process natural language replies.5. Frequency CappingLogic: Admins set global limits (e.g., max 4 emails per 30 days). Subscribers reaching this limit are marked as capped and skipped.Fail-open: If the capping service fails, the system defaults to "Allow Send" to ensure critical communications are not blocked.Force Send: Managers can override limits for urgent or mandatory announcements during the approval stage.Technical ArchitectureFrontend (HTML/CSS/JS): Static pages, Subscriber management UI, Campaign management, Analytics dashboards, Approval workflows.Backend (Node.js): REST API (Express), JWT Authentication, Mail scheduling, Tracking services, File upload services.Database (MS SQL): Subscriber data, Newsletter templates, Delivery logs, Statistical data.Project StructurePlaintextWintonEDM/
